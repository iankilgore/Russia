Mongol rule

Mongols rule Russian cities (Kiev, Moscow, Vladimir), though they allow Russian princes to retain political power as long as they pay tribute (tax revenue)
Mongol capital is established long the Volga River Batu Khan named the city Sarai Batu
Batu's brother makes Islam the official religion of the Mongols, mosques and bath houses are built

Traditional Mongol social structure was based on the family and it was a patriarchy


Mongols were nomadic wanderers of the land that formed clans, then larger tribes

When tribes started to unite, the strongest leader was known as Khan (a monarchical title) The most famous and strongest Khan? Genghis Khan (1162-1227) conquered much territory around northern China and fathered so many children, it is believed that approximately 1 of every 200 or so Chinese and Mongolian men are indirectly related to Genghis Khan

The Mongols owed their military success to a few characteristics: mobility, organization, and ferocity

Mongol Characteristics

Mobile:
Besides being nomadic and thus invulnerable to any attack on their 'homeland', Mongol soldiers were also expert fighters on horseback
Mongol soldiers were expected to maintain at least three horse for battle
Organized:
Commanders in the Mongol army knew the value of when to attack en masse (all at once) and when to 'divide and conquer'
Mongol troops could attack in a large, coordinated way or they could break into small groups that would encircle and ambush foes... This flexible was a massive advantage
Ferocity:
Mongol military leaders were masters of psychological warfare, deception, and terror. Their tactics ranged from offering cities a chance to avoid being ransacked by paying tribute before an attack... Knowing an established town would not be willingly burned or abandoned When the Mongols did destroy a city, they allowed a few citizens to remain alive to tell the story of the brutality of what had transpired.

Fall of the Tsars and Revolution

  • (1894-1917) last czar: Nicholas II and the Romanovs
  • There was great social upheaval and change in Europe and Russia during the reign of Nicolas II
  • The Russian social democratic Labour Party (RSDL) formed in the 1890s' but split in the early 20th century
  • By 1905, half of the RSDL became the radical Bolshevik Party and more moderate Menshevik party
  • Bolsheviks were founded and led by Vladimir Lenin and Alexander Bogdanov
  • Both groups believed in socialism, but the Bolsheviks were willing to use violent revolution to achieve their means.

BLOODY SUNDAY

  • Revolution of 1905/"Bloody Sunday": crowd of Bolsheviks led by father Capon, March to the Winter Palace to air grievances to the tsar
  • Cossack soldiers open fire, killing hundreds. Russian people responded with a labor strike and social revolts
  • Czar Nicolas consents to a legislative group, the Duma, to be formed and voting rights for members of the Duma to be an essential part of lawmaking
  • Moderate Mensheviks are appeased but the violent Bolsheviks demand more dramatic change.

ON TO COMMUNISM

  • Following WWI, workers began striking across Russia in February
  • By July, the tsar abdicates in the effort to appease the revolutionaries
  • The tsar and his family are abducted and then executed in 1918, though there is continual myth about whether or not Nicolas' daughter, Anastasia, survived and was hidden away.
  • The Duma and the leaders of the Bolsheviks, Vladimir Lenin and Leo Trotsky, attempt to take control, but civil war breaks out
  • Communist leaders emerge victorious, and the Soviet Union is established

The first party chairperson is Joseph Stalin, who would become the absolute dictator of the USSR until his death in 1953.
